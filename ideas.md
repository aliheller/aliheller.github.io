# Ali Heller Personal Website — Design Ideas

## Context
Ali Heller is an Assistant Professor of Anthropology at the University of Maryland, a qualitative researcher in West Africa, and a documentary photographer. Her work centers on reproductive health, obstetric fistula, and humanitarianism in Niger. The site needs to reflect scholarly authority, warmth, and a documentary/journalistic visual sensibility.

**Design Movement:** Editorial Documentary — inspired by long-form journalism publications (The New Yorker, Aperture, Magnum Photos)

**Core Principles:**
- Typography-first hierarchy: text carries the visual weight, not decoration
- Photographs are given full breathing room — large, unhurried, respected
- Restrained palette that never competes with the subject matter
- Academic credibility balanced with human warmth

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: warm off-white `#F5F0EB` — like aged paper, not clinical white
- Primary text: deep charcoal `#1C1A18`
- Accent: dusty terracotta `#B5522A` — evokes West African earth tones, warmth
- Secondary: slate grey `#6B6560`
- No gradients; color is used sparingly as punctuation

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric editorial grid: wide left column for text, narrow right for pull quotes and metadata
- Hero section: full-bleed photograph with name overlaid in large serif type
- Section dividers are thin horizontal rules, not cards or boxes
- Photography gallery: masonry-style, images at varying sizes

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin horizontal rule separators with small typographic ornaments
- Pull quotes in italic serif, offset to the right margin
- Section labels in small-caps, widely letter-spaced

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover on nav: underline slides in from left
- Image hover: subtle scale (1.02) with a slow ease
- Page transitions: fade-in on scroll, no jarring jumps

**Animation:**
- Entrance: elements fade up 20px with 0.6s ease-out on scroll
- No looping animations; motion is purposeful and brief

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif) — for name, section titles, book title
- Body: Source Serif 4 — readable, humanist, academic
- Labels/UI: DM Sans — clean, neutral counterpoint
- Hierarchy: 72px name / 36px section titles / 18px body / 12px labels

## Selected Approach

**Quiet Modernism** — warm linen background, Cormorant Garamond display type, Lora body, sage green accents. This reflects Ali's work: scholarly but deeply human, visually rich but never sensational. The generous whitespace honors the gravity of her subject matter, while the warm palette echoes the West African context of her research.
